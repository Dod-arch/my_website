A bit about me

The unusual path that got me here.

I've been writing professionally since 2014 — first as a language editor for STEM books (Cambridge University Press, Wiley Blackwell), then as a content editor at IDP Education, then through a long stretch in content marketing, and now as a technical writer at Whatfix.

At Whatfix, I own documentation for AI-first product features, SSO and authentication, and in-app guidance workflows. I'm involved early — sitting in on design reviews, doing UAT testing, joining customer calls — because good documentation starts before the feature ships, not after.

I've contributed to documentation that directly supported over $27M in ARR. Not as a vanity metric — but because self-service documentation genuinely reduces support load and unblocks adoption. That's the work I care about.

I'm curious about accessibility and usability writing, information architecture, and the gap between what documentation says and what users actually need. I occasionally write about this on Substack.
